# Writing Standards

Applies to any written output produced on my behalf: commit messages, PR descriptions, code comments, README files, client-facing copy, docs.

## Hard rules

- No em dashes
- No buzzwords or filler language (e.g. "leverage," "unlock," "game-changer," "seamless")
- No cliche phrases or metaphors
- No "not just X but Y" constructions
- No overly structured or templated writing. No forced 3-part frameworks, no "Here's the thing:" openers, no listicle cadence unless the content is genuinely a list

## Voice

- Write as a record of an actual decision, not a take aimed at engagement
- Precision over reach. Say the specific thing that happened, not the general lesson wrapped around it
- Craft in application. Show the work, not commentary about the work
- Quiet confidence, not persuasion. No selling the reader on how impressive something is

## Sentence-level

- Short, direct sentences. Cut qualifiers that soften without adding meaning
- Concrete nouns and verbs over abstract ones ("skipped the Places API" not "made a strategic technical choice")
- No rhetorical questions as transitions
- No "imagine if" or "picture this" openers
